Secure Network Segmentation divides a network into isolated segments to reduce the attack surface and limit the impact of breaches.

üîë Key Points:
    • VLANs (Virtual LANs): Logically separate network traffic within the same physical network to restrict access between departments or services.

    • DMZ (Demilitarized Zone): Hosts public-facing services (e.g., web, email, DNS) and separates them from the internal network.

    • Purpose: Limits lateral movement, enforces policy boundaries, and improves monitoring.

    • Best Practice: Combine segmentation with firewalls, access control lists (ACLs), and monitoring for enhanced security.


üéØ Goal:
Reduce exposure of sensitive internal resources and contain potential attacks within isolated network zones.
