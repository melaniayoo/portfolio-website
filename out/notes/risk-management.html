Risk Management

August 14, 2025
2 min read
Risk management involves identifying, analyzing, responding to, and monitoring risks that could impact an organization's operations or objectives.

    Types:
    • Ad Hoc: Done when needed.

    • Continuous: Ongoing monitoring.


    üßÆ Risk Analysis


    Qualitative: Subjective, high-level evaluation (likelihood + impact).

    Quantitative: Numerical evaluation using metrics:

    • SLE: Expected loss per event.

    • ARO: Estimated frequency per year.

    • ALE: Annual expected loss (SLE √ó ARO).

    • Exposure Factor: Percentage of asset lost.

    • Impact: Effect on financial, operational, or reputational areas.

    • Risk Appetite: Amount of risk an organization is willing to accept.

    • Mitigate: Reduce likelihood or impact.

    üîé Risk Monitoring


    Ongoing tracking of risks and effectiveness of responses.
